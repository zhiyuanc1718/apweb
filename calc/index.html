Limits

Limits Notation

$$\lim _{x\to c}f(x)=L$$

Limits at Infinity

$$\lim _{x\to 1}{\frac {x^{2}-1}{x-1}}=2$$

What exactly are Limits

The general idea of a limit is the y value that a function approaches when it approaches a certain x value. This is the expected y value for a function, the value does not have to be defined.

 

Derivatives

Disguised Derivatives

$$f'(a)=\lim _{h\to 0}{\frac {f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}}$$

Mathematical Definition

$$m={\frac {\Delta f(a)}{\Delta a}}={\frac {f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}}$$

What are Derivatives

The slope of the Tangent line at a specific point is called the derivative. In other words, it is the rate of change at an instantaneous moment. Unlike the average rate of change between two points on a graph, finding the slope at one point requires more work, and this is why we need to learn some calculus to find the value.

 

Integrals

General Integral

$${\displaystyle \int _{a}^{b}\,f(x)dx=\left[F(x)\right]_{a}^{b}=F(b)-F(a)\,}$$

How are Integrals different

Integrals help us determine the area under a curve. While derivatives find the change, integrals are antiderivatives: they find what was being differentiated. These terms might sound fancy or confusing but Calculus is here to help you understand.
