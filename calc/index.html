Course Description

Prerequisite

AP Calculus requires you to understand concepts across geometry, algebra and trigonometry. You should memorize the unit circle and formulas of different shapes (spheres, trapezoids etc).

  • Surface Areas of 3D shapes
  • Areas of trapezoids, triangles & etc
  • Volumes and Trigonometry Identities

AP Exam

The exam is given within the first weeks of May. You have September to May to prepare for this exam. Receiving a passing score on the exam might give you college credits. Scores range from 1 to 5. A 3 is considered a passing score.

  • 2019 May 14 Tuesday Exam
  • 2020 May 5 Tuesday Exam

AP Exam Format

The AP Calculus AB Exam is 3 hours and 15 minutes long and has a 2 multiple choice sections and 2 free response sections.

Section I — Part A: Multiple Choice | 30 Questions | 60 minutes | No Calculator | 33.3% of Exam Score

  • Question topics range from limits to integrals.
  • You have to do all math without a calculator.
  • You have two minutes per question so it is NOT recommended that you spend more than 2 minutes on a question
  • It is reasonable to say that difficulty increases as you proceed towards the end. However, this is not confirmed to be true, so skip around questions as you wish.

Section I — Part B: Multiple Choice | 15 Questions | 45 minutes | Calculator | 16.7% of Exam Score

  • Question topics range from finding derivatives to integrals
  • You may not require the calculator for some questions.
  • Because you have three minutes per question, you will feel less stressed out on this section if you proceed at regular pace.

Section II — Part A: Free Response | 2 Question | 30 minutes | Calculator Required | 16.7% of Exam Score

  • This section you have 15 minutes per question
  • Because the Section II booklet will not be sealed up, you can go back to this section later
  • However, you will not be able to use the calculator once 30 minutes is up.
  • All Answers must be rounded to three decimal places at the end of calculation.

Section II — Part B: Free Response | 4 Question | 60 minutes | No Calculator | 33.3% of Exam Score

  • You will have to perform integrals by hand (including complex ones)
  • Answers do not have to be simplifed unless asked by the question
  • You have 15 minutes per question and you are free to skip around
  • Each question is worth nine points
  • You will answer questions in parts A, B , C and even D.
  • [Tip] : You are advised to make up answers for parts you do not know the answer to.

What exactly are Limits

The general idea of a limit is the y value that a function approaches when it approaches a certain x value. This is the expected y value for a function, the value does not have to be defined.

Difficulty

Disguised Derivatives

$$f'(a)=\lim _{h\to 0}{\frac {f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}}$$

Mathematical Definition

$$m={\frac {\Delta f(a)}{\Delta a}}={\frac {f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}}$$

What are Derivatives

The slope of the Tangent line at a specific point is called the derivative. In other words, it is the rate of change at an instantaneous moment. Unlike the average rate of change between two points on a graph, finding the slope at one point requires more work, and this is why we need to learn some calculus to find the value.

What are Integrals

Integrals help us determine the area under a curve. While derivatives find the change, integrals are antiderivatives: they find what was being differentiated. These terms might sound fancy or confusing, but Calculus is here to help you understand.
